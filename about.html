The Story

The shop opened
on a quiet promise.

Hicksville Optical opened on Newbridge Road in 1998 with one rule taped to the back of the front-desk computer: don't rush the exam. The handwriting has faded, the computer has been replaced four times, and the rule is still there.

The practice has stayed independent on purpose. No corporate quotas, no upsell scripts, no "premium package" pushed at checkout. The frame wall changes when something better shows up at a trade show, not when a sales rep brings croissants.

"Don't rush the exam."

The patients have stayed too. Three-generation families now — grandparents who came in 1998, parents who came as teenagers, kids walking in on their own. Some still ask if the same little waiting-room lamp is the same lamp from twenty years ago. It is.

What stays the same

Four rules. Same as ever.

01

Don't rush the exam.

A 60-minute new-patient exam is 60 minutes — not 22 with a "we're booked" excuse.

02

Don't sell the catalogue.

No commission-driven upsell. If the $185 frame fits your face better than the $485 one, we say so.

03

Don't hide the price.

Every frame on the wall has a tag.

04

Don't forget the name.

When you walk in, we know your name and what you tried last time.
